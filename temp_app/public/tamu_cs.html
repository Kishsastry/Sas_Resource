STEP 1: Freshman Admission to General Engineering

You are not admitted directly to Computer Science as a freshman. You must first be accepted into the College of Engineering as a "General Engineering" major. Admission is holistic, considering multiple factors.

For Texas Residents

Automatic University Admission

If you are in the top 10% of your Texas high school class, you are automatically admitted to TAMU. You must still be reviewed for placement into the competitive College of Engineering.

STEP 2: Entry to a Major (ETAM)

After completing 1-2 years of foundational engineering, math, and science courses, you apply for your specific major. This is the most critical step for getting into Computer Science.

ETAM Holistic Review: Your Profile

If your GPA is below 3.75, you are not out! The CS department will review your application holistically. This is where your passion projects and extracurriculars become extremely important.

  • Cumulative GPA: Still the most important factor.
  • Passion Projects: Built an app? Competed in a hackathon? Have a GitHub profile? This proves your interest and aptitude beyond classes.
  • Research: Any research with a professor (especially in CS) is highly valued.
  • Extracurriculars: Leadership in CS-related student organizations (e.g., TAMUHack, Aggie Coding Club) is a major plus.
  • AP/IB Credits: Strong grades in foundational courses (Math, Physics, ENGR) are key. AP credits help you start strong.

STEP 3: Adding the "And Business"

Once you are accepted into the Computer Science major, you can officially add a business component. This is not part of your initial admission but is managed after you are an established student.

Option 1: Business Minor

The most common path.

The Mays Business School offers a Business Minor open to non-business students. It requires 18 credit hours in foundational business courses (accounting, finance, marketing, management).

Option 2: Double Major

More complex, high-credit load.

Pursuing a double major in CS and a business field (like Finance) is possible but requires careful planning with academic advisors from both colleges. It will likely take more than 4 years to complete.
