A Visual Guide to UT Austin Computer Science Admissions

The CS Admission Funnel: A Competitive Landscape

Getting into UT Austin is tough, but gaining admission to the Computer Science program is a separate, even more competitive challenge. You are first admitted to the university, and then your chosen major. The CS program has a significantly lower acceptance rate than the university's overall rate.

The Two Pillars of Admission: A Holistic Review

UT Austin uses a "holistic review," meaning they look at your entire application, not just one or two numbers. Your application is evaluated on two primary pillars.

Pillar 1: Academic Excellence

This is the foundation. It proves you can handle the rigorous, fast-paced curriculum of a top-tier CS program.

Pillar 2: Holistic Proof

This is your "X-Factor." It demonstrates who you are beyond your grades and proves your genuine passion for computer science.

Pillar 1: Deconstructing Academic Excellence

While there's no "magic number," the academic profile of an admitted CS student is exceptionally strong. This includes your rank, the difficulty of your courses, and (if submitted) your test scores.

The Auto-Admit "Myth" for CS

Being in the top 6% of your Texas high school class grants you automatic admission to UT Austin, but **not** to the CS major. You still face a highly competitive review for your major.

Course Rigor: What They Look For

Admissions wants to see you've taken the hardest STEM courses your school offers and excelled.

  • ✔ Calculus-Ready: AP Calculus (AB or BC) is a virtual prerequisite.
  • ✔ AP CS A: Shows foundational knowledge and interest.
  • ✔ AP Physics C: Both (E&M and Mechanics) are highly regarded.
  • ✔ Other STEM APs: AP Chemistry, AP Biology, AP Statistics.
  • ✔ 4 Years of Math & Science: A non-negotiable expectation.

Recommended Test Scores (If Submitted)

While UT is often test-optional, submitting high scores (especially in Math) can significantly strengthen your application for a competitive STEM major like CS.

Pillar 2: Building Your Holistic Proof

This is where you show your passion. What have you done *outside* of class that proves you love to build, solve problems, and think like a computer scientist?

Extracurriculars: Depth > Breadth

Admissions would rather see deep commitment and leadership in one or two CS-related activities than shallow participation in ten unrelated clubs.

The "Proof of Passion" Portfolio

This is your single biggest differentiator. What have you built?

  • 💻 Passion Projects: A personal website, a mobile app, a game, a data analysis, a script to automate a task. (Link your GitHub!)
  • 🏅 Competitions: USACO, FIRST Robotics, CyberPatriot, local hackathons.
  • 🔍 Research: Participation in a university lab or a formal research project is extremely impressive.
  • 💼 Work/Internship: A CS-related summer job or internship.

Essays: Your Personal Narrative

This is where you tie everything together. Your essays (especially the short answers) are your chance to *explain* your passion. Don't just list your projects; explain the *why*. What problem did you want to solve? What did you learn? How did it change your perspective? This is your chance to show your voice and personality.
